blog post 3

November 5, 2024

For my personal style blog, I want to go with a very cute and fun aesthetic, while using a pastel, muted, minimalist color palette. Some sites I found that do a great job of balancing the crafty and modern look are Twirling Pages and Krist Yu.

For the former, I like the idea of a border with a bunch of tiny videos or pictures above the header, giving the website a more personal touch. The latter really appealed to me in terms of the “scrapbook” feel that it brought to the viewer, along with the minimalist color palette.

The third site I found was Nikki Lo. I really enjoyed the clean look of large pictures being the focal point and then the story underneath each picture. It even inspired me for my main blog page to do a similar layout. One thing that I think could be improved about this site is the width. I feel as if the screen layout is really wide and it is hard to focus on one thing at a time.

Lastly, I thought The Every Girl blog was a good site for inspiration on the content of my blog, finding your personal style. I like the collage look that the header brings, along with the fun but muted colors. One thing that I felt could have been improved was the margin sizing when you scroll down to the content, as there are a bunch of ads taking up the left half of the page and make the blog feel cluttered. Overall, I think I’ve found some good examples for what I do and do not like from a personal blog.
