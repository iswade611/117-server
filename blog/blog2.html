Semantic HTML is useful not only for the coder and designer to tell where all the different sections of a website or article are located, but also for those using the website that are blind or visually impaired. For my first blog post, I chose to use different types of semantic HTML such as header tags, navigation tags, article tags, and footer tags. All of these different tags will help both me and my user to easily access my blog post. With the header being the main title of the post, the article tag being the main story that Iâ€™ve written about, and the footer being at the bottom of the post with my name and contact information, the elements give a simplistic but clean structure of my content.

Styling that I experimented with was different placement of the buttons on the page to navigate between the various blog posts, home page, next post, and previous post. The most challenging part in terms of visual design was definitely designing the home page. I was having a lot of trouble with spacing in terms of where each blog post was going to sit on my home page, and I ended up changing around my design a couple of times to get the look that I wanted.
